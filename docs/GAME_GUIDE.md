## Introduction

Love Letter is a game of risk, deduction, and luck for 2-8 players. Players attempt to deliver their love letter to the Princess while removing others from the game. The game is played in several quick rounds, with the winner of each round receiving a token of affection.

## Game Overview

**Objective**: Win tokens by being the last player standing in a round or having the highest-valued card when the deck runs out.

**Duration**: 10-20 minutes

**Players**: 2-8 players

**Components**:
- 16-24 cards (depending on player count)
- Tokens of affection (hearts)

---

## Game Setup

### Starting a Game

1. **Create or Join a Room**
   - Host creates a game and receives a 6-digit room code
   - Other players join using the room code
   - No login required (guest mode available)

2. **Player Count**
   - Minimum: 2 players
   - Maximum: 8 players
   - Different player counts use different deck compositions

3. **Tokens to Win**
   - 2 players: 7 tokens
   - 3 players: 5 tokens
   - 4 players: 4 tokens
   - 5-8 players: 3 tokens

### Round Setup

At the start of each round:
- All players receive **one card**
- One card is **set aside face-down** (2-player games only)
- The remaining cards form the **draw pile**
- The first player begins their turn

## The Cards

There are 8 different card types, each with a unique value and effect:

| Card | Value | Quantity (2-4p) | Quantity (5-8p) | Effect |
|------|-------|-----------------|-----------------|--------|
| **Princess** | 8 | 1 | 2 | Lose if discarded |
| **Countess** | 7 | 1 | 2 | Must discard if caught with King or Prince |
| **King** | 6 | 1 | 2 | Trade hands with another player |
| **Prince** | 5 | 2 | 3 | Choose any player to discard their hand and draw new |
| **Handmaid** | 4 | 2 | 3 | Protection until your next turn |
| **Baron** | 3 | 2 | 3 | Compare hands; lower value is eliminated |
| **Priest** | 2 | 2 | 3 | Look at another player's hand |
| **Guard** | 1 | 5 | 6 | Guess another player's card |

## How to Play

### Turn Structure

Each turn follows these steps:

1. **Draw a Card**
   - Draw one card from the deck
   - You now have two cards in hand

2. **Choose and Play a Card**
   - Select one of your two cards to play
   - Discard it to the discard pile
   - Apply the card's effect (if applicable)

3. **End Turn**
   - Keep your remaining card
   - Next player's turn begins

### Special Rules

**Mandatory Discard**: If you hold the Countess (7) and either the King (6) or Prince (5), you **must** discard the Countess.

**Protection**: Players protected by the Handmaid cannot be targeted by any card effects until their next turn.

**Elimination**: Eliminated players sit out for the rest of the round but return for the next round.

## Card Effects in Detail

### üë∞ Princess (8)
**Effect**: If you discard this card, you are immediately eliminated.

**When to Play**:
- When you have no other choice (e.g., forced by Prince effect)
- Never voluntarily discard the Princess!

**Strategy**: The Princess is the strongest card. Try to keep it until the end of the round.

---

### üíÉ Countess (7)
**Effect**: If you have the King or Prince in your hand, you must discard the Countess.

**When to Play**:
- Automatically when you have King or Prince
- As a bluff to make others think you had a King or Prince

**Strategy**: Sometimes revealing you have the Countess gives away information, so be strategic.

---

### üëë King (6)
**Effect**: Trade your hand with another player of your choice.

**When to Play**:
- When you have a low-value card and want to swap
- To steal what you suspect is a high-value card
- To force someone to take your Countess

**Strategy**: Great for obtaining the Princess or forcing opponents to eliminate themselves.

**Important**: Remember the Countess rule - if you draw a Countess while holding a King, you must immediately discard the Countess.

---

### ü§¥ Prince (5)
**Effect**: Choose any player (including yourself) to discard their hand and draw a new card.

**When to Play**:
- To force suspected Princess holders to discard
- To refresh your own hand when stuck with a low card
- To eliminate players who discard the Princess

**Strategy**: Risky but powerful. Can instantly eliminate the Princess holder.

**Special Case**: If the deck is empty, the targeted player draws the set-aside card (2-player game) or draws nothing and is eliminated.

**Important**: If you force yourself to discard and draw the Countess while holding the Prince you just drew, you must immediately discard the Countess.

---

### üë∏ Handmaid (4)
**Effect**: Until your next turn, you cannot be targeted by any card effects.

**When to Play**:
- When you have a high-value card and want to protect it
- When you suspect others might target you
- To buy time and gather information

**Strategy**: Excellent defensive card. Use it to protect the Princess or other high cards.

**Limitation**: If all other players are protected by Handmaids, you must choose yourself as a target (if the card requires a target).

---

### üé© Baron (3)
**Effect**: Choose another player. Both players reveal their hands. The player with the lower value is eliminated.

**When to Play**:
- When you have a high card (6, 7, or 8)
- After using Priest to know what someone has
- Against players who discarded low cards

**Strategy**: Information is power. Use the Priest before playing Baron for guaranteed elimination.

**Tie**: If both cards are equal, nothing happens.

---

### ‚õ™ Priest (2)
**Effect**: Look at another player's hand secretly.

**When to Play**:
- Early in the round to gather information
- Before playing Baron or Guard
- To track who might have the Princess

**Strategy**: Knowledge is crucial. Use this to inform your later decisions.

**Remember**: Keep the information to yourself! Don't reveal what you saw.

---

### üõ°Ô∏è Guard (1)
**Effect**: Choose another player and name a card (not Guard). If they have that card, they are eliminated.

**When to Play**:
- When you have information from Priest
- To eliminate suspected Princess holders
- As a educated guess based on discards

**Strategy**: Most common card. Use deduction based on:
- Cards already played
- What cards people might logically hold
- Your own hand

**Restriction**: You cannot guess "Guard" (too easy).

## Winning the Game

### Winning a Round

A round ends when:

1. **Only One Player Remains**
   - All other players have been eliminated
   - The remaining player wins the round

2. **Deck Runs Out**
   - All players reveal their hands
   - Highest card value wins
   - Ties are broken by the sum of discarded cards (if tracked)

### Winning the Round = Token

The winner of each round receives **one token of affection** (‚ù§Ô∏è).

### Winning the Game

The first player to collect the required number of tokens wins the game:
- **2 players**: 7 tokens
- **3 players**: 5 tokens
- **4 players**: 4 tokens
- **5-8 players**: 3 tokens

## Strategy Tips

### General Strategy

1. **Information is Power**
   - Use Priests to scout opponents
   - Track cards that have been played
   - Deduce what cards are still in play

2. **Card Counting**
   - Remember what's been discarded
   - Calculate probabilities of remaining cards
   - Adjust strategy based on what's left

3. **Timing Matters**
   - Play defensively early (Handmaid)
   - Play aggressively late (Baron, Guard)
   - Save high cards for the end when possible

4. **Target Selection**
   - Eliminate strong players first
   - Target those who've played few cards
   - Avoid protected players

### Advanced Tactics

**Bluffing with Discards**
- Discard Countess to make others think you have King/Prince
- Use this to manipulate opponent decisions

**The Princess Dilemma**
- If you have the Princess, play defensively
- Use Handmaids to protect it
- Be wary of Prince effects

**Baron Combos**
- Use Priest ‚Üí Baron combination for guaranteed kills
- Only play Baron when you're confident

**Guard Deduction**
- Eliminate impossible cards based on discards
- Narrow down options systematically
- Make educated guesses, not random ones

**King Manipulation**
- Swap bad cards for good ones
- Force opponents into difficult situations
- Use to avoid the Countess rule

## Common Scenarios

### Scenario 1: You Have Princess + Handmaid
**Best Play**: Play the Handmaid to protect your Princess. This buys you a turn and prevents anyone from forcing you to discard.

---

### Scenario 2: You Have King + Countess
**Best Play**: You must discard the Countess (mandatory). This reveals to others that you had a King, so expect to be targeted.

---

### Scenario 3: You Used Priest and Saw Princess
**Best Play**:
- If you have Baron: Play it on that player to eliminate them
- If you have Prince: Force them to discard the Princess
- If you have Guard on your next turn: Call Princess to eliminate them

---

### Scenario 4: Only 2 Cards Left in Deck
**Best Play**: Consider holding onto higher cards. The round is likely to end with comparison rather than elimination.

---

### Scenario 5: Everyone Else is Protected by Handmaid
**Best Play**:
- If your card requires a target, you must target yourself
- Play a card that doesn't require targeting (Handmaid, Countess)
- Wait until next turn when protections expire

---

### Scenario 6: You Have Prince + Prince
**Best Play**: Use Prince on yourself first to draw a new card. This is risky but can improve your position, especially late in the round.

## Quick Reference Chart

### Card Priority (When to Discard)

**High Priority to Keep:**
1. Princess (8) - Almost never discard
2. King (6) - Great for swapping
3. Baron (3) - Good if you have high card
4. Handmaid (4) - Excellent protection

**Medium Priority:**
5. Prince (5) - Situational power
6. Priest (2) - Information gathering

**Low Priority:**
7. Guard (1) - Common, use often
8. Countess (7) - Must discard if with King/Prince

### Targeting Priority

**High Priority Targets:**
- Players with few discarded cards (likely has good hand)
- Players who played Countess (might have King/Prince)
- Players close to winning (most tokens)

**Low Priority Targets:**
- Players with many low-value discards
- Players far behind in tokens
- Protected players (impossible)

## Summary

Love Letter is a game of:
- **Deduction**: Figure out what cards opponents hold
- **Risk Management**: Know when to play it safe vs. aggressive
- **Bluffing**: Mislead opponents about your hand
- **Timing**: Play the right card at the right moment
- **Luck**: Sometimes the draw decides everything

**Remember**: Every round is a fresh start. Even if you're behind, you can catch up quickly!
